Answers to the questions we hear most. If yours isn't here, [send us a message](/contact/).

<div class="faq-list">

  <details class="faq-item">
	<summary class="faq-question">What makes these matches different from standard cigar matches?</summary>
	<div class="faq-answer">
	  <p>Three things: thickness, burn time, and finish. Standard cigar matches are thin enough to snap when you strike them and burn out before you've finished lighting. These are 3× thicker — they won't break on the strike — and they burn slow and even for up to 60 seconds. That's enough time to light a cigar properly, with one match, without rushing.</p>
	  <p>They're also hand-finished with a Slojd knife, which gives each match a slightly irregular, tapered profile. It's not purely decorative — that profile helps the match seat cleanly between your fingers and gives you a more controlled strike.</p>
	</div>
  </details>

  <details class="faq-item">
	<summary class="faq-question">How do I use a long cigar match?</summary>
	<div class="faq-answer">
	  <p>Strike away from your body on the striker strip provided. Let the sulfur head burn off for a second or two before bringing the flame to the foot of the cigar — you'll taste the difference. Rotate the cigar slowly as you light, keeping the flame just below the leaf rather than touching it directly. The long burn time means you don't have to rush.</p>
	  <p>For more detail on the technique, see the <a href="/cigar-101/">Cigar 101</a> page.</p>
	</div>
  </details>
